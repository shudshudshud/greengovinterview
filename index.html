PSSO's Mission & GCSO Vision

Understanding the Public Sector Sustainability Office's role in orchestrating Singapore's ambitious net-zero transformation

The Conductor's Role

As GCSO Lim Tuang Liang describes it: "The policy planners compose the music, individual agencies play their sections, and as the conductor, I oversee the different parts to ensure coordination and harmony."

Key Insight: PSSO's value isn't in dictating what agencies do, but in enabling coordination across diverse missions and resource constraints.

Three Pillars of GreenGov.SG

Excel: Ambitious sustainability targets with measurable outcomes

  • Net zero emissions by 2045
  • 10% reduction in energy and water use by 2030
  • 30% reduction in waste disposal by 2030

Enable: Embedding sustainability into government operations

  • Green procurement standards by 2028
  • Sustainable touchpoints and programs

Excite: Building culture and capability

  • Officer awareness and knowledge
  • Workplace sustainability actions

Current Challenges

Based on GCSO insights and FY2023 report findings:

  • Diverse Agency Missions: Not all agencies have sustainability as primary focus
  • Resource Constraints: Limited budget and talent for sustainability initiatives
  • Cross-Agency Coordination: Challenges require collaboration beyond single agency scope
  • Data Quality: Inconsistent measurement and reporting standards

Continuous Discovery Approach

Systematic method for generating, testing, and scaling product ideas through rapid stakeholder feedback

Weekly Discovery Cycle

Monday-Tuesday: Stakeholder Conversations
Continuous conversations with PSSO team, agency officers, and technical teams to identify pain points and opportunities
Wednesday: Insight Synthesis
Consolidate findings, identify patterns, and prioritize opportunities based on impact and feasibility
Thursday: Rapid Prototyping
Build clickable prototypes, mockups, or process flows that demonstrate core concept
Friday: Stakeholder Feedback
Test prototype with PSSO team and key stakeholders, gather feedback for iteration or termination

Idea Evaluation Framework

Systematic criteria for advancing ideas from prototype to pilot:

Advance to Pilot if:
  • Solves clear pain point for 5+ agencies
  • Reduces manual work by 20+ hours/month
  • Positive feedback from 3+ stakeholder groups
  • Technical feasibility confirmed
Kill if:
  • Limited stakeholder interest
  • High technical complexity, low impact
  • Duplicates existing solutions
  • Requires significant behavior change

Pilot to Production

Scaling successful prototypes through structured pilot programs:

Week 1-2: Pilot Design
Define success metrics, identify pilot agencies, establish feedback loops
Week 3-8: Pilot Execution
Deploy with 3-5 agencies, gather usage data, iterate based on feedback
Week 9-10: Stakeholder Buy-in
Present results to PSSO leadership, secure resources for full rollout

High-Priority Product Ideas

Focused on practical solutions that drive measurable impact on sustainability outcomes

Automated Compliance & Reporting Engine

Streamline the eGreenGov reporting process by automating data collection from building management systems, procurement databases, and facility management tools. Reduce manual reporting time while improving data accuracy and consistency.

Impact: 50-75% reduction in reporting time, 95% data accuracy, 100% on-time submissions

Smart Building Integration Platform

Connect with existing building management systems to automatically optimize energy usage, predict maintenance needs, and reduce waste. Focus on government facilities with highest emissions footprint.

Impact: 20-30% energy reduction, S$50M+ annual savings, automated reporting to eGreenGov

Green Procurement Intelligence

Integrate with GoBusiness platform to help procurement officers identify sustainable alternatives, calculate lifecycle assessments, and ensure compliance with green procurement standards.

Impact: 100% green procurement compliance, 25% increase in sustainable suppliers, S$100M+ sustainable procurement annually

Cross-Agency Resource Exchange

Digital marketplace for agencies to share underutilized resources, coordinate bulk purchasing, and collaborate on sustainability projects. Reduce duplicate procurement and maximize asset utilization.

Impact: 30% reduction in duplicate procurement, S$20M+ cost savings, 75% agency participation

Public Accountability Dashboard

User-friendly interface that makes GreenGov.SG reports easier to navigate and understand. Show progress toward targets without exposing individual agency performance in ways that could be misinterpreted.

Impact: 500K+ annual visits, 85% public satisfaction, improved transparency without agency risk

Supporting FY2024 & FY2025 Reporting

Strategic approach to improving annual GreenGov.SG reports while building foundation for future automation

FY2024 Report (Due End 2025)

Planning Phase

Focus on understanding current process rather than major changes:

  • Shadow current report compilation process
  • Identify data quality issues and bottlenecks
  • Document stakeholder feedback from FY2023 report
  • Map agency submission patterns and challenges
Key Insight: Use FY2024 as learning opportunity to understand system constraints and user needs

FY2025 Report (Due End 2026)

Priority Focus

Opportunity to implement process improvements and automation:

  • Automated data collection from pilot agencies
  • Improved data validation and quality checks
  • Enhanced public-facing dashboard and visualizations
  • Streamlined agency submission process
Success Metrics: 50% reduction in manual work, 95% data accuracy, enhanced public engagement

Current Reporting Landscape

GreenGov.SG Annual Reports:

  • FY2022: Inaugural report (Dec 2023)
  • FY2023: Added waste data, improved categorization
  • 100+ agencies reporting through eGreenGov portal

Statutory Board Disclosures:

  • Individual sustainability reports (started FY2024)
  • Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions reporting
  • Examples: MUIS, BCA, Land Surveyors Board
